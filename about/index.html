About

I’m Jaan, a self taught programmer. At the moment I’m trying to get into app development by learning native Android. After years of aimlessly learning random programming languages for fun, now I’m trying to commit to one and make useful programs.

I started coding after graduating high school, after I got curious about Linux from the show Mr. Robot. The first months, I’ve probably distro-hopped more than my fingers could count, using my neighbor’s WiFi to download ISOs (sorry!), trying to find the best one and ended up settling with where I started anyway, Ubuntu, because it’s the easiest to install. Right now I’m a happy user of KDE Neon.

This is my blog. For now I don’t have any specific topics I’m planning to write about and I’ve mainly created a blog for three reasons. The first one is that I wanted to take on the 100 Days to Offload challenge I keep reading about in Mastodon, the second is that I’m in desperate need of another hobby besides coding. And the third is to incentivize myself to learn more and have something interesting to share about.

Toot me at @bredlof if you have anything to share!
