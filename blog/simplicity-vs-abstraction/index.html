Simplicity vs Abstraction

Feb 10 2021 · Ramblings

Simplicity and abstraction isn’t the lack of complexity, it is a way to deal with complexity.

Complexity is when something is made up of many entities and have innumerable interactions between each other, to such a degree that it is difficult to keep track of them consciously at once.

Simplicity is complexity organized and extraneous things omitted, which makes the navigability of entities and interactions a lot easier.

Abstraction is hiding complexity, and only showing what is relevant to a certain use case or situation at the cost of losing capability.

Simplification requires that you know all the entities and interactions involved in order to organize them effectively, while abstraction only requires that you know what you need, hence abstraction is a lot easier than simplification.

Simplification and abstraction are not mutually exclusive, often you will use both.

This rambling is inspired by my experiences in programming and made clearer by Jonathan Blow’s Preventing the Collapse of Civilization talk.
